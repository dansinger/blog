The birth of the blog

14 November 2015

I set out to write for a half an hour every day in November, but I’ve completely failed at that. That’s alright. I’ve done some other stuff that I’m pretty happy about.

For one, I managed to get this blog up and running. I’ve been wanting to get into blogging for a while: to have some place to put my thoughts and ideas as a way of actually improving the quality my thoughts and ideas. I tried Medium, but always felt this urge to write “We believe X, Y, and Z…” and “We should…,” when really I had no place speaking for anyone else. It’s a beautiful platform for sharing ideas, but it doesn’t feel particularly right for me. I want to say something without trying to say capital-s “Something.”

I looked at some other options, including svbtle.com, which I find beautiful, but couldn’t justify the monthly expense for this little writing experiment (which, as demonstrated, is not easy for me). Eventually, I settled on ghost.org, a simple yet elegant open-source blogging platform. There are options for cloud hosting, but I was able to follow a combination of a number of guides to run Ghost on GitHub pages. It uses a tool called Buster to brute force the blog onto a static page, which you can then push to GitHub. I had to learn some git stuff and troubleshoot a lot, which mostly consisted of uninstalling and reinstalling nodejs a thousand times.

It’s still the default theme, so that needs some work, but I was pretty satisfied when I got it on GitHub. (Satisfied is an understatement, I guess –– I literally threw my hands into the air and yelled.)

So, the next step was…actually having a GitHub page in order to link my blog. I’ve been meaning to get a personal website up for a ridiculously long amount of time. Ridiculous, because:
1. I know I can do it, and
2. Creating a github.io page is free.
I have three or site structures and css files from ages past — all failed attempts to capture what I wanted. These sites had awesome, high-resolution images and plenty of crazy responsive stuff, but were never finished.

No duh.

Every day at work, I extol the virtues of “knowing the problem you’re trying to solve” and “understanding your audience” and most significantly, “just do something!” And yet, I managed to completely ignore all of those. Easily, in fact. I want a place to showcase my content, to share my ideas, and to show my design sensibilities: I’m not trying to be a web developer. So, this morning, I just shut up and did it.

I sat on the couch, and in a matter of a couple hours, I was able to whip up the little site you see on dansinger.github.io. It’s not fancy, and some of the pages are missing, but I did it and got it onto the internet. That’s a huge accomplishment, and I am really proud. It’s simple, looks decent on mobile, and I did it by myself.

So, dear reader (probably just the one),

Watch this space. I’m going to continue to work hard to chronicle my efforts to develop into a functioning person, and in the spirit of the internets, will be sharing the products of those efforts here. I look forward to eventually being embarrassed by myself for (hopefully) years to come.

Dan Singer
